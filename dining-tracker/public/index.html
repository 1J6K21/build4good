    AGGIE HONOR

    CODE OF HONOR

    "An Aggie does not lie, cheat or steal or tolerate those who do."

    SBISA FACT

    Oldest Dining Hall

    Did you know Sbisa Dining Hall opened in 1912? It was named after Bernard Sbisa, the college's first steward.

    TRADITION CORNER
    • SILVER TAPS: Held the first Tuesday of the month for students who passed.
    • MUSTER: April 21st. Softly call the roll of those absent.
    • ELEPHANT WALK: Seniors say goodbye to campus.
    CENTURY TREE

    Oldest Aggie Tradition

    The legendary Century Tree stands near the academic building. It is said that if two people walk under it together, they will eventually marry.

    QUICK TIPS
    • Gig 'em!
    • Early birds get the best eggs at Sbisa.
    • Don't forget your sports pass.
    • Stand for the 12th Man.
    YELL PRACTICE

    MIDNIGHT YELL

    Held at Kyle Field before home games. Join the Yell Leaders to get the stadium rocking. Warm up your pipes!

    CORPS OF CADETS

    The Keeper of the Spirit

    Established in 1876, the Corps remains the largest uniformed student body in the nation outside the service academies. Proud members of the 12th Man.

    REVEILLE

    MISS REVEILLE X

    The First Lady of Aggieland. If she barks in class, the professor is expected to dismiss the lecture immediately!

    THE AGGIE RING

    A Symbol of Unity

    The most recognized collegiate ring in the world. It represents a promise to serve the state and nation. Gig 'em!

    GIG 'EM ORIGINS

    Homer Norton, 1930

    Coach Homer Norton started the "Gig 'em" tradition at a yell practice. It's now the universal sign of Aggie approval.

    AGGIE VOCAB

    Good Bull

    Phrase used to describe anything that follows the Aggie Code of Honor or shows great spirit.
